Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

1. Who We Are

Veriyo is a South African consumer transparency platform that allows vehicle owners to share and access information about car repair pricing at workshops across South Africa. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, store, use, and protect your personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA), the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002 (ECT Act), and other applicable laws of the Republic of South Africa.

For all privacy-related enquiries, contact us at: privacy@veriyo.co.za

2. What Information We Collect

We collect different information depending on how you use Veriyo.

When you submit a repair report:

  • Workshop name, suburb, and city
  • Vehicle make, model, and year
  • Date of repair
  • Type of repair and part or service description
  • Amount quoted and amount paid
  • Your rating and additional notes
  • An invoice image, if you choose to upload one

When you list a workshop:

  • Workshop name and physical address
  • Contact number and email address
  • Operating hours and specialisations
  • Service pricing information
  • Years in operation and accreditation status
  • Approximate location coordinates, if you use the "Use My Location" auto-fill feature

When you claim an existing workshop listing:

  • Proof-of-ownership or verification documents you choose to upload (for example, a business registration document or ID)
  • Any other information you submit to support your claim

When you use in-platform chat (motorist–workshop or partner–admin):

  • The content of messages you send
  • The date and time messages are sent
  • Your account identifier, so messages can be matched to the correct conversation

When you join or use the Veriyo Partner Programme:

  • Full name, email address, and phone number
  • Referral activity linked to your unique partner code (clicks, submissions, and workshop sign-ups originating from your link)
  • Task completion and activity statistics used to calculate programme status and estimated earnings

General browsing and referral tracking (all visitors):

  • A randomly generated visitor identifier stored in your browser, used to attribute a visit to a partner's referral link
  • Which repair-price submissions or workshop listings you have referred to on this device (used only to let you access your own referred entries again, e.g. saved workshops)

3. Why We Collect It

We collect this information to build a transparent, community-driven record of vehicle repair pricing in South Africa; to allow motorists, workshops, partners, and Veriyo administrators to communicate directly through the platform; to operate the Partner Programme, including tracking referrals and calculating earnings; to verify claims of workshop ownership; and to keep the platform secure and functioning correctly.

We do not use your information for marketing. We do not sell your information to any third party. We do not use your information for any purpose beyond what is described in this policy.

4. Who We Share Your Information With

We use a small number of third-party services to operate this platform, some of which are based outside South Africa. Where information is transferred outside South Africa, we take reasonable steps to ensure it remains subject to a level of protection consistent with POPIA's requirements for cross-border transfers (section 72 of POPIA). By using Veriyo, you acknowledge that your information may be processed in these jurisdictions.

Supabase Inc (United States)

All submission, listing, chat, and account data is stored on Supabase's database and file storage infrastructure, including any documents uploaded during a workshop claim. Supabase operates under applicable United States data protection standards.

ntfy.sh (Germany)

When a new submission is received, a notification is sent through ntfy.sh to alert the platform administrator. This notification contains the workshop name, city, repair type, and amount paid from your submission. It does not contain your personal identity.

OpenStreetMap Nominatim

If a workshop owner uses the "Use My Location" feature when listing a workshop, the device's coordinates are sent to OpenStreetMap's Nominatim service to auto-fill an address. Veriyo does not store your precise coordinates — only the resulting suburb, city, and province are saved to the listing.

5. Published Information and User-Generated Content

Submissions that are approved by our moderation team are displayed publicly on the Browse Prices page. Published submissions include the workshop name, suburb, city, vehicle details, repair type, amount paid, rating, and notes. We do not publish any information that identifies you personally as the person who submitted the report.

All published submissions, ratings, and notes represent the personal experiences and opinions of the individual motorists who submitted them. Veriyo does not independently verify the factual accuracy of individual submissions, and does not endorse, adopt, or take responsibility for the content of any submission, rating, or chat message posted by a user. Published content should be read as community experience reports, not as independently verified facts.

Veriyo acts as an intermediary "hosting" service as contemplated in section 75 of the ECT Act, and does not screen content before submission. If you believe a submission, rating, note, or chat message is false, defamatory, infringes your rights, or is otherwise unlawful, you may report it to privacy@veriyo.co.za with details of the content and the reason for your complaint. We will review reported content and may remove or restrict it at our discretion. Removal of content is not an admission that it was inaccurate or unlawful.

6. In-Platform Chat

Veriyo provides direct messaging between motorists and workshops, and between partners and Veriyo administrators, to make it easier to resolve queries. Messages are stored so that both sides of a conversation can see its history, and are visible only to the participants in that conversation and to authorised Veriyo administrators.

The system automatically checks outgoing messages for patterns resembling phone numbers or email addresses and blocks them, so that contact details are not exchanged off-platform. This filter is a best-effort technical measure only; it may not catch every attempt to share contact details, and Veriyo cannot guarantee that no personal information will ever be exchanged through chat. You remain responsible for what you choose to type and send. Veriyo is not a party to, and accepts no responsibility for, any arrangement, dispute, or agreement that participants make with each other through chat.

7. Invoice Images and Verification Documents

If you upload an invoice image with a repair submission, it is stored securely and used only to support the review of your submission. Invoice images are not published publicly. If your submission is approved, only the information you entered into the form fields is displayed — not the image itself.

If you upload verification documents to claim ownership of a workshop listing, those documents are stored securely and used only to assess your claim. They are not published or made publicly accessible, and are only viewed by authorised Veriyo administrators for the purpose of processing the claim. Veriyo takes reasonable steps to assess the genuineness of a claim but cannot guarantee that every claim is legitimate, and accepts no liability for loss arising from a fraudulent or mistaken claim that passes our review process.

8. Cookies, Local Storage, and Similar Technologies

Veriyo does not use advertising or third-party tracking cookies. We do use your browser's local storage — a technology similar to a cookie that stores small amounts of information on your own device — for functional purposes only: remembering a referral code so a partner is correctly credited, generating an anonymous visitor identifier for referral-click tracking, remembering which workshops you have saved for later, and remembering when you last read a chat conversation so unread messages can be highlighted. This information stays on your own device and is not something Veriyo can read directly from other websites you visit. You can clear it at any time through your browser settings, though doing so may reset saved workshops or referral attribution.

9. How Long We Keep Your Information

Repair submissions are kept for as long as they remain relevant to the platform's purpose of providing pricing transparency to South African consumers. Workshop listings are kept until the business requests removal or until the listing is no longer accurate. Chat messages are kept for as long as the associated account or conversation remains active on the platform, so that conversation history remains available to its participants. Workshop claim verification documents are kept only for as long as reasonably needed to assess and record the outcome of the claim. You may request deletion of your information at any time by contacting us at the address in section 1.

10. Your Rights Under POPIA

The Protection of Personal Information Act gives you the following rights regarding your personal information held by Veriyo:

  • Right to access — you may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to correction — you may request that inaccurate information be corrected
  • Right to deletion — you may request that your personal information be deleted from our records
  • Right to object — you may object to the processing of your personal information, including its use for a purpose you did not originally consent to

Veriyo does not make any decision based solely on automated processing that would result in profiling or have a legal or similarly significant effect on you, as described in section 71 of POPIA. Moderation decisions and claim reviews on Veriyo are made or confirmed by a human administrator.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at privacy@veriyo.co.za. We will respond within a reasonable time and in any event within 30 days.

11. Data Security and Security Compromises

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect the personal information we hold, including restricting administrator access, applying access-control rules at the database level, and using industry-standard infrastructure providers. No system can be guaranteed 100% secure, and Veriyo cannot accept liability for unauthorised access that occurs despite these reasonable measures.

In the event of a security compromise that has, on reasonable grounds, resulted in unauthorised access to your personal information, we will notify the Information Regulator and affected users as required by section 22 of POPIA. If you become aware of any security concern related to your information on this platform, contact us immediately at privacy@veriyo.co.za.
